Definition

Contract that gives one the right to either buy or sell an underlying asset at a predefined price in the future.
There exist two types of options: call and put.


Glossary
  • Exercise an option

    • Action of applying an option.


  • Call option

    • Option that allows one to buy an underlying for a predetermined price in the future.


  • Put option

    • Option that allows one to sell an underlying for a predetermined price in the future.


  • Writer

    • Individual that has the obligation to sell or buy an underlying the option is written on if the option is exercised.

Product
  • European options
  • American options
European options

Options that one can exercise only at an agreed date called maturity date.

American options

Options that one can exercise at any time before an agreed date called maturity date.
